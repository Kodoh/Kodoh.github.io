Research Overview

I study proof complexity with a focus on the power and limitations of Pseudo-Boolean proof systems and Cutting Planes. These systems form some of the most expressive and geometrically meaningful approaches to propositional reasoning.

Much of my work relates to lower bounds, automatability questions, and connections between algebraic and geometric viewpoints on proofs. In particular, I am interested in the complexity of refuting Boolean formulas using linear inequalities.
